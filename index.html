Week 1: Basic Elements of a Medical Word

    • all medical words consist some/all these elements: wood root; combining form; suffix; prefix.

WORD ROOT

    • medical term foundation, establishes main definition
    • all medical terms have at least 1 word root
    • most medical words derived from Greek/Latin → 2 diff. word roots w/ same meaning
    • Greek word root: describe condition, diagnosis, disease, treatment
    • Latin word root: describe anatomical structure

  English Term     Greek Word Root     Latin Word Root     Greek Word Analysis     Latin Word Analysis  
  skin   dermatos   cutis   dermat/itis: skin inflammation     cutane/ous: pertaining to skin  
  kidney   nephr   ren   nephr/oma: kidney tumor   ren/al: pertaining to kidney  
  mouth   stomat   or   stomat/itis: mouth inflammation     or/al: pertaining to mouth  

COMBINING FORM

    • created when word root combined w/ vowel
      EX) erythr + o = erythr/o
    • word root = erythr, meaning red
    • word root combines w/ combing vowel → form combing form erythr/o
    • Combining Vowel: vowel link word root; usually an o, sometimes an i
    • has no meaning on its own
    • enables connection between 2/more word elements
  Word Root     Combining Form     Meaning  
  erythr   erythr/o   red
  gastr   gastr/o   stomach
  hepat   hepat/o   liver
  immun   immun/o   immune
  nephr   nephr/o   kidney
  oste   oste/o   bone
    • a combining form links 1 root to another root → form compound word
  Combining Form     Word Root     Suffix     Medical Word  
  oste/o: bone   chondr: cartilage     -itis: inflammation     oste/o/chondr/itis: bone & cartilage inflammation  
  oste/o: bone   arthr: joint   -itis: inflammation   oste/o/arthr/itis: bone & joint inflammation

SUFFIX

    • word element placed at end of word → alters meaning
    • all medical terms have suffix
    • usually describes pathology (abnormality/disease), symptom, surgical/diagnostic procedure, part of speech
    • word root links to suffix if suffix begins w/ vowel
      EX) hepat + itis = hepat/itis
    • word root = hepat, meaning liver
    • word root combines w/ suffix -itis, meaning inflammation
    • suffix -itis begins w/ vowel i, hence suffix links to word root
    • when word root hepat & suffix -itis combine, medical word hepat/itis means liver inflammation
    • combining form links suffix if suffix begins w/ consonant
      EX) hepat + o + cyte = hepat/o/cyte
    • word root = hepat, meaning liver
    • suffix = -cyte, meaning cell
    • suffix -cyte begins w/ consonant c, needs combing vowel after word root → hepat turns into combining form hepat/o
    • when combing form hepat/o & suffix -cyte combine, medical word hepat/o/cyte means liver cell

  Suffix     Medical Word     Rationale  
  -itis: inflammation     gastr/itis: stomach inflammation     suffix starts w/ vowel, word root links suffix  
  -megaly: enlargement     gastr/o/megaly: stomach enlargement     suffix starts w/ consonant, combining form links suffix  
  -oma: tumor     hepat/oma: liver tumor     suffix starts w/ vowel, word root links suffix  
  -cyte: cell     hepat/o/cytes: liver cells     suffix starts w/ consonant, combining form links suffix  
  -pathy: disease     pancreat/o/pathy: pancreas disease     suffix starts w/ consonant, combining form links suffix  

PREFIX

    • word element attached word root beginning
    • not all medical terms have prefix
    • indicate number, time, position, direction, negation

  Prefix     Word Root     Suffix     Medical Word  
  an-: without     esthes: feeling     -ia: condition     an/esthes/ia: condition of not feeling  
  hyper-: excessive     therm: heat     -ia: condition     hyper/therm/ia: excessive heat condition  
  intra-: within     muscul: muscle     -ar: pertaining to     intra/muscul/ar: pertaining to within muscle  
  para-: near     nas: nose     -al: pertaining to     para/nas/al: pertaining to near nose  
  poly-: many     ur: urine     -ia: condition     poly/ur/ia: excessive urine condition  
  pre-: before     nat: birth     -al: pertaining to     pre/nat/al: pertaining to before birth  

Basic Guidelines

    Defining Medical Word
    1. Define word last part (suffix)
    2. Define word first part (word root/combining form/prefix)
    3. Define word middle part (word root)
      EX) gastr/o/enter/itis
      1. last part of word = suffix -itis, meaning inflammation
      2. first part of word = prefix gastro-, meaning stomach
      3. middle part of word = combining form enter, meaning intestine
    • when combine suffix -itis, combining form gastro-, & word root enter altogether, medical word gastr/o/enter/itis means stomach & intestines inflammation

Pronunciation

    Macron: diacritical mark ¯ indicates long vowels sound
    Breve: diacritical mark ˘ indicates short vowels sound
    • combination ch sometimes pronounced k
      EX) cholesterol, cholemia
    • when pn at word beginning, pronounced only w/ n sound
      EX) pneumonia, pneumotoxin
    • when pn in word middle, p pronounced
      EX) orthopnea, hyperpnea
    • when i at word end, pronounced like eye
      EX) bronchi, fungi, nuclei
    • for ae & oe, only second vowel pronounced
      EX) bursae, pleurae


Week 2: Suffixes

    • suffix added to word root/combining form end → changes medical word meaning
    • suffix begins w/ vowel → use word root for linking 2 word elements
    • suffix begins w/ consonant → use combining form for linking 2 word elements
